## Problem

Every B2C scale-up eventually hits the same wall: a revenue metric moves, the dashboard shows it moved, and nobody can cleanly answer why. The analyst team gets pulled off roadmap work for two weeks of ad-hoc SQL. The answer, when it lands, is neither reproducible nor owned by anyone.

I lived this for ten years at scale. I built the tool I wanted to exist.

## Approach

MetricPilot is a Revenue Root Cause Engine wrapped in a fixed-scope consulting engagement. You don&rsquo;t buy a license — you buy an outcome.

The technical spine:

- **KPI decomposition** — additive driver analysis. The metric moved by X. Driver A contributed +Y, driver B contributed −Z.
- **Change-point detection** — Theil-Sen slopes and STL-based decomposition to isolate *when* and *where* the break happened (cohort, channel, platform, geography).
- **Self-serve workbook** — delivered as a Streamlit app your team runs against your Snowflake warehouse forever after.

A public demo-factory repo runs the full pipeline on proxy data from Club Med&rsquo;s repeat-guest rate — deterministic, auditable, ready to show before we engage.

## Outcome

The [precursor system I built](/case-studies/tripadvisor-precursor) at TripAdvisor (the &ldquo;Business Metric Wizard&rdquo;) cut KPI investigation time by 80% across five product teams. The downstream Insight Bot scaled to 500+ self-service queries per month.

MetricPilot takes that same engine, extracts the general logic, and ships it as a packaged engagement any B2C data team can buy without a sales call.
