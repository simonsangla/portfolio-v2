## Problem

Most people use ChatGPT like a smarter Google. They type a question, skim the answer, close the tab. That extracts maybe 10% of what the tool can do for a knowledge worker.

The gap is not intelligence or access — it is operator fluency. Knowing when to role-prompt, how to iterate instead of re-ask, when to switch models, how to chain outputs, when to stop and rewrite the instruction.

## Approach

**Make ChatGPT Your Slave** is a productized 90-minute 1:1 engagement. You do not buy a workshop — you buy an operating system for the tool.

The spine:

- **Pre-session intake** — you send me two or three real weekly workflows (writing, research, review, planning) ahead of the call.
- **Live rebuild** — on the call, we rebuild those workflows together. You watch the iteration happen on the first one; you drive the second half.
- **Named methodology** — role-framing, iteration loops, output chaining, model routing (ChatGPT vs Claude vs local). Every move is named so you can repeat it.
- **Playbook handoff** — you leave with a custom prompt playbook and a one-page model-routing cheatsheet, both tailored to the workflows we rebuilt.
- **Async follow-up** — 30-minute slot two weeks later to debug what broke when you applied it at your desk.

## Outcome

Ten years of analytics at TripAdvisor scale taught me the bottleneck on a knowledge worker is rarely the tool — it is the interface they built in their head to talk to the tool. This session rebuilds that interface, and the playbook locks in the rebuild so it does not decay.
