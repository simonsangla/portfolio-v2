The problem

Scale-ups rarely lack data. They lack a framework for asking why a number moved that outlives the person who last looked at it. Analysts churn, dashboards rot, and six months later the CEO is asking the same question.

The approach

Every engagement starts the same way: read-only access to your warehouse, a written intake, a target KPI. From there the engine decomposes the metric into its additive drivers and runs change-point detection per driver.

  • Schema assessment — what we have, what's clean, what needs backfill
  • Decomposition model — fitted to your actuals, validated against a held-out month
  • Change-point report — by cohort, channel, platform
  • Streamlit dashboard — the diagnostic becomes self-serve

What you keep

Everything versioned in your repo. A runbook.md that your next analyst can follow without me. The dashboard runs on your Snowflake warrant — I have no keys after day 28.

The point isn't to explain one quarter. It's to leave your team a tool that explains the next one without calling me.
